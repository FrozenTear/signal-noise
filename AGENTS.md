# Signal Noise — Editorial Team Operating Rules

This file codifies the editorial policies that govern the Signal Noise pipeline.
It applies to every agent in the editorial team: Scanner, Source Checker (Pipeline),
Reporters (Quill/Bolt/Muse/Ember), Article Verifier, and the Editor-in-Chief.

The Editor-in-Chief is the final gatekeeper before publication and owns enforcement
of these rules. See `docs/SCANNER.md` for the discovery stage of the pipeline.

## Article Volume Policy

- **Target:** 10 articles/day in week 1. Ramp to 15/day once the combined
  Article Verifier + Source Checker pass rate is **≥ 90% over the prior 3 days**.
- **Per-beat cap:** Max 3–4 articles per beat — Linux (Quill), Tech (Bolt),
  Privacy (Muse), Climate (Ember).
- **H2H pairs count as 2** against the daily cap.
- **Empty beat-slots are allowed.** Better a 12-article day than a 15-article day
  padded with filler. Do not manufacture stories to hit the number.

## Mandatory Pipeline

Every article MUST pass, in order:

1. **Source Checker verification** — before a draft is written.
2. **Article Verifier** — before the article is published.

No article is published that has skipped either gate.

## Kill Quota

The Editor-in-Chief targets a **≥ 20% kill rate on greenlit drafts per week.**
If nothing is killed in a week, the editing bar is not high enough — tighten it.
Kills are content: every kill ships with a clear, honest, funny rejection note.

## Transparency

A **weekly retro** publishes that week's kills and corrections as content.
Readers see what we rejected and why, and what we got wrong and fixed.
